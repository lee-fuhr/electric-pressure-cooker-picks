Quick answer

The Instant Pot Duo 7-in-1 Electric Pressure Cooker is the best overall pick for most households, offering a perfect balance of performance, ease of use, and value.

The Ninja Foodi 9-in-1 is ideal if you want an air fryer combo, while the Breville Fast Slow Pro suits those who want precise cooking controls and are willing to invest more.

Electric pressure cookers have become a kitchen staple because they turn tough cuts of meat tender in under an hour, cook dried beans without soaking, and simplify one-pot meals from soups to rice. They’re energy-efficient, safer than stovetop models, and many double as slow cookers or steamers, making them a versatile tool for busy cooks. When choosing an electric pressure cooker, focus on capacity (most families do well with a 6‑quart model), the number of programmable functions (more isn’t always better, look for the ones you’ll actually use), and ease of cleaning (a stainless‑steel insert with dishwasher‑safe parts is a big plus). Consider your counter space and whether you value extra features like a built‑in air fryer or true sous‑vide mode.

Best for specific needs

Best for meal prep

If you batch-cook beans, grains, and soups every week, the Instant Pot Duo 7-in-1 is your workhorse. Its large 6-quart capacity fits a full pound of dried beans or a whole chicken, and the delay-start timer lets you set it up before work. The simple presets and quick pressure release make prepping multiple batches fast and consistent. Our pick: Instant Pot Duo 7-in-1 Electric Pressure Cooker.

Best for families on a budget

The Cuisinart CPC-600N1 costs less than many multi-function models while still delivering reliable pressure cooking for family-sized meals. Its straightforward controls mean anyone in the household can use it, and the stainless-steel pot is tough enough for daily use. Skip the frills and save your money. Our pick: Cuisinart CPC-600N1 6-Quart Electric Pressure Cooker.

Best for small kitchens

The Ninja Foodi 9-in-1 may seem large, but it replaces a pressure cooker and an air fryer – saving you from storing two separate appliances. If counter space is tight, this combo machine earns its keep. Just be prepared to store the extra lid when not in use, or dedicate a shelf for the second lid. Our pick: Ninja Foodi 9-in-1 Pressure Cooker and Air Fryer.

Our verdict

For nearly everyone, the Instant Pot Duo 7-in-1 remains the best buy: it’s reliable, easy to use, and backed by a huge community. If you need an air fryer combo, the Ninja Foodi does that brilliantly, while the Breville Fast Slow Pro delivers unmatched precision for serious cooks. The Cuisinart is a fine budget choice, and the Instant Pot Duo Plus gives you sous-vide without a steep price hike. Pick the one that fits your cooking style, and you’ll wonder how you ever lived without a pressure cooker.
