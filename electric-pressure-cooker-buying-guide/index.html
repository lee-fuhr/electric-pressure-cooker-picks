How to choose an electric pressure cooker

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

An electric pressure cooker is a countertop appliance that combines pressure cooking, slow cooking, steaming, and more into one pot. For 2026, the best pick for most households is a 6-quart multi-cooker from a reputable brand with at least ten preset programs and a clear manual pressure valve. Focus on capacity, safety features, and ease of cleanup rather than gimmicks.

How much capacity do I need?

Capacity is the first decision because it determines how much you can cook at once. A 3-quart model is compact and great for side dishes, rice, or serving one to two people. A 6-quart cooker is the sweet spot for most couples and small families, it fits a whole chicken or several pounds of stew meat without taking over your counter. An 8-quart or larger model suits meal preppers, big families, or anyone who wants to batch-cook roasts and soups. Keep in mind that a pressure cooker must be filled at least halfway to reach proper pressure, so a 6-quart pot is not ideal for just a single cup of rice. A 3-quart model handles small batches better. Also consider storage: bigger cookers are heavier and need more cabinet space. If you cook for one or two most days, a 3- or 6-quart is plenty; go bigger only if you regularly feed a crowd or freeze leftovers.

What are the essential functions to look for?

The core function is pressure cooking, but most modern electric pressure cookers are multi-cookers that also slow cook, sauté, steam, and make rice and yogurt. Do not feel pressured to use every preset, most people rely on pressure cook, sauté, and slow cook. Look for a model that lets you adjust the pressure level (high/low) and has a dedicated steam release valve that is simple to operate. A useful extra is a built-in delay timer, so you can load ingredients in the morning and have dinner ready when you walk in. Keep-an-warm mode is also common and handy. The real differentiator is the inner pot: a stainless steel pot is preferable because it browns well, does not scratch, and lasts longer than non-stick linings. Non-stick pots are easier to clean but prone to peeling over time. Choose stainless steel unless you have a specific need for non-stick.

Are electric pressure cookers safe to leave unattended?

Modern electric pressure cookers are designed with multiple safety systems that make them very safe for unattended cooking while you are at home. They include lid-locking mechanisms that prevent opening while pressurized, pressure sensors that cut off heat if pressure exceeds safe limits, and overheat protection. These features make them far less risky than older stovetop models. That said, no appliance should be left on in an empty house for many hours. Plug the cooker directly into a wall outlet (avoid extension cords) and keep the steam vent clear. The cooking program automatically switches to keep warm when the timer ends, so even if you forget, the pot holds food at a safe temperature without continued pressure. Common sense applies: if you are home and awake, you can safely start a recipe and walk away.

Which brands are most reliable in 2026?

Instant Pot remains the dominant brand and the one most recipes are developed for. Their models have a proven track record, strong customer support, and a wide range of accessories. For 2026, the Instant Pot Duo or Pro lines are solid choices if you want a model that works with nearly any pressure cooker recipe you find online. Other brands worth considering include Ninja (their Foodi pressure cookers add a crisping lid for air frying), Cosori (user-friendly controls and good warranty), and Breville (higher-end build quality, though they cost more). When choosing a brand, look at the availability of replacement parts like sealing rings and the length of the warranty. A brand that sells parts directly and offers at least a one-year warranty gives you peace of mind.

What is the difference between stovetop and electric pressure cookers?

Stovetop pressure cookers reach higher pressures (typically 15 psi) and come to pressure faster, which means shorter cooking times for things like beans or stocks. They also get hotter for better browning and caramelization. However, they require you to monitor the heat on a burner and manually release pressure, they are not set-and-forget. Electric pressure cookers operate at a slightly lower pressure (around 10 to 12 psi) and take longer to heat up and depressurize. But they automate the entire process: you set the timer, and the machine controls the heat and shifts to keep warm automatically. They also include built-in safety features that make them more approachable for beginners. If you want convenience and a multi-function device that does more than just pressure cook, go electric. If you want the fastest possible cooking and a rugged all-metal pot, consider a good stovetop model.

How do I choose between a basic model and a smart model?

A basic electric pressure cooker has physical buttons, a simple display, and uses no Wi-Fi. It is often budget-friendly, reliable, and easy to operate. These models do everything you need: pressure cook, slow cook, sauté, and steam. You don’t have to worry about firmware updates or app connectivity. Smart models add Wi-Fi connectivity, allowing you to monitor and adjust cooking from your phone, browse recipes, or download new programs. The convenience is real if you like starting dinner remotely or following step-by-step guided recipes. However, smart features add cost and complexity. If you are comfortable using a timer and a recipe book, a basic model is the wiser choice. Only upgrade to a smart cooker if you really value app integration and expect to use the guided cooking features regularly.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use an electric pressure cooker for canning?

No, most electric pressure cookers do not reach the sustained high pressure required for safe home canning of low-acid foods. For canning, you need a stovetop pressure canner or a dedicated electric canner.

How long does it take to cook beans from dry?

Dried beans generally cook in 20 to 40 minutes under pressure, depending on the variety and whether they were pre-soaked. Expect similar times across reputable models.

Is it worth spending more on a stainless steel inner pot?

Yes, stainless steel is more durable and easier to clean than non-stick, and it won’t chip or peel. It also works better for browning and sautéing.

Can I use an electric pressure cooker as a rice cooker?

Absolutely. Most multi-cookers have a rice program that works well for white rice, though results may be slightly less precise than a dedicated rice cooker for some varieties.

What is the typical lifespan of an electric pressure cooker?

With regular use and proper care, a quality electric pressure cooker should last 5 to 10 years. The gasket and valve parts need periodic replacement.

How does pressure cooking affect the nutritional value of food?

Pressure cooking preserves nutrients well because it cooks quickly with minimal water and a sealed environment, similar to steaming. It is considered a healthy cooking method.

In shortChoosing an electric pressure cooker comes down to matching your kitchen space, cooking volume, and desired features. Stick with a well-reviewed 6-quart model from a trusted brand, and you’ll have a versatile tool that saves time and simplifies meal prep for years.
